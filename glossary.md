# VELOCITY Glossary

Key terms and concepts from all 36 essays, alphabetically sorted. Each entry includes the source essay(s) where the concept appears and a brief working definition.

## A

### Acción sin Permiso
**Definition:** Acting without waiting for validation, approval, or perfect conditions.
**Use when:** You're waiting for someone to give you permission to start.
**Source:** Multiple essays including "Lanzar es solo el Preparatorio", "Permiso Retroactivo"

### Alianza / Alianzas que Suman Dimensiones
**Definition:** Partnership based on complementary perspectives, not audience overlap.
**vs:** Alianza por afinidad (similar people, creates duplication).
**Principle:** Watson + Crick discovered DNA because each saw what the other couldn't. Dimension addition > audience addition.
**Source:** "Con quién te alías define quién eres"

### Ambición sin Culpa
**Definition:** Pursuing your goals without apologizing, diminishing, or qualifying them.
**Use when:** You're reducing your ambition to fit in or make others comfortable.
**Source:** "Ambición sin culpa"

### Anti-Pattern
**Definition:** A decision or behavior that looks productive but destroys value over time.
**Examples:** Optimizing instead of shipping, planning instead of testing, perfecting instead of launching.
**Source:** All essays; see "La falacia del cartógrafo", "La ilusión de competencia"

### Asimetría de Información
**Definition:** When one party in a transaction knows more than the other about quality/value.
**Consequence:** Without signals, the best players leave the market.
**Source:** "El mercado de los limones"

## F

### Fijación Funcional
**Definition:** The tendency to perceive objects only according to their usual function.
**The Problem:** Once you label something "container of tacks," you can't see it as a "holder."
**Principle:** Categories organize perception—and limit it.
**Application:** Question the labels on everything in your business. What else could they be?
**Source:** "Fijación funcional + Trabajo viejo vs trabajo nuevo + Certeza ciega"

## G

### Gastos Lineales
**Definition:** Expenses that produce linear results (same cost = same output forever).
**Examples:** Offices, equipment, recurring subscriptions, normalized processes.
**Problem:** They become invisible once normalized and drain without multiplying.
**vs:** Inversiones que multiplican.
**Source:** "Gastos que normalizas & Disciplina que no cuestionas"

## B

### Burnout (Quemarse)
**Definition:** The state where effort no longer produces growth; you're grinding without compounding.
**Recognition:** When activity feels high but results feel stagnant.
**Source:** Multiple essays on effort, tension, and sustainable execution

## C

### Cambio de Fase
**Definition:** The moment when the rules of the game shift and old strategies stop working.
**Recognition:** What worked before suddenly doesn't. New competitors emerge. The market changes shape.
**Source:** "Cambio de fase", "Falacia del jugador"

### Cicatrices (Scars)
**Definition:** Failures, struggles, and setbacks that become evidence of your real experience in the arena.
**Use when:** You're hiding your failures or treating them as shame.
**Source:** "Tus cicatrices son credenciales"

## I

### Inversiones que Multiplican
**Definition:** Expenses that create disproportionate returns (one investment → multiple results).
**Examples:** Strategic coaching, high-leverage tools, system architecture, product development.
**Principle:** The question isn't "Can I afford it?" It's "Does this multiply or just sum?"
**Source:** "Gastos que normalizas & Disciplina que no cuestionas"

## C

### Competencia (Competence)
**Definition:** Actual demonstrated skill through repeated execution.
**vs:** Understanding (which requires only thought, no action).
**Source:** "La ilusión de competencia"

### Certeza Ciega
**Definition:** Being so certain of your narrative that you go blind to contradicting signals.
**Recognition:** You know exactly what works, so you can't see what's actually happening.
**Principle:** Doubt isn't indecision—it's the capacity to see more variables.
**Source:** "Fijación funcional + Trabajo viejo vs trabajo nuevo + Certeza ciega"

### Compounding
**Definition:** Results that build on previous results; each iteration makes the next one easier.
**vs:** Linear effort (same effort = same results every time).
**Source:** Multiple essays on execution, repetition, and long-term strategy

### Cuello de Botella (Bottleneck)
**Definition:** A single point of passage through which everything must flow.
**Problem:** When you ARE the bottleneck, your business can't scale beyond your capacity.
**Solution:** Design a system that doesn't depend on a single point of passage.
**Source:** "Facturas como empresario, decides como freelance"

## D

### Decisión (Decision)
**Definition:** A choice that includes removing your escape routes and committing fully.
**vs:** Choosing (picking one option while keeping others open).
**Source:** "Elegir vs Decidir"

### Deriva Invisible
**Definition:** Slow, imperceptible shift away from your core purpose through small compromises.
**Recognition:** One day you wake up doing something completely different from what you intended.
**Source:** "Deriva invisible"

## E

### Efecto Zajonc
**Definition:** Repeated exposure increases preference and familiarity.
**Use when:** You're trying to build awareness or change perception.
**Limitation:** Exposure only works if you show up repeatedly.
**Source:** "Efecto Zajonc"

### Efecto Green Lumber
**Definition:** The best insights come from focusing on what others consider boring or basic.
**Use when:** You're tempted by complexity but simplicity would solve the problem.
**Source:** "El efecto Green Lumber"

### Elegir vs Decidir
**Definition:** The distinction between choosing (picking) and deciding (committing).
**Key difference:** Deciding includes removing escape routes. Choosing leaves them open.
**Source:** "Elegir vs Decidir"

### Entropía Organizativa
**Definition:** The tendency of organizations to become disorganized unless energy is actively applied.
**Use when:** Your team is losing focus or structure without clear cause.
**Source:** "Deriva invisible"

### Escalera (Ladder)
**Definition:** The metaphorical support structure you lean on in early growth.
**Principle:** You must burn it to move to the next level.
**Source:** "Tirar la escalera"

## F

### Falacia del Cartógrafo
**Definition:** Spending time mapping instead of exploring; treating the map as if it were the territory.
**Cost:** Every day spent mapping is a day someone else is exploring.
**Source:** "La falacia del cartógrafo", "Dragones en el mapa"

### Fase (Phase)
**Definition:** A distinct period with its own rules, dynamics, and required capabilities.
**Examples:** Phase 1 = solo founder; Phase 2 = team; Phase 3 = organization; Phase 4 = institution.
**Source:** "Cambio de fase", "Lanzar es solo el Preparatorio"

### Fricción
**Definition:** Resistance in a system that slows progress or prevents movement.
**Use:** Can be a filter (good) or a barrier (bad). Knowing which matters.
**Source:** "La fricción es el filtro"

## G

### Ganador (Winner)
**Definition:** Someone who moves; who crosses the threshold when it appears.
**Trait:** Winners create, observers analyze.
**Source:** "Los que crean vs observan"

## I

### Ilusión de Competencia
**Definition:** Feeling skilled because you understand something intellectually, confusing theory with practice.
**Danger:** This is where most people live; they know but don't do.
**Source:** "La ilusión de competencia"

## J

### Juego (The Game)
**Definition:** The current context, rules, and dynamics of your market or life.
**Principle:** The game continues. Rules change. Players come and go.
**Source:** "El juego continúa"

## L

### Lanzar Imperfecto
**Definition:** Shipping 70% done, letting the market teach you what 100% looks like.
**vs:** Perfect delivery (which takes 10x longer and usually aims at the wrong target).
**Source:** "Lanzar imperfecto construye imperios"

### Liderazgo Invisible
**Definition:** Leadership through influence, not authority. Making others powerful, not yourself big.
**Measure:** How much more capable is your team because you're here?
**Source:** "Liderazgo invisible"

### Ley de Amara
**Definition:** People overestimate what can be done in 1 year, underestimate what's possible in 10.
**Use:** For long-term strategy; compound time beats intensity.
**Source:** "Ley de Amara"

### Ley de Brandolini
**Definition:** Bullshit takes 1 unit of energy to create, 100 units to refute.
**Implication:** Don't waste energy on noise. Let wrong ideas die from neglect.
**Source:** "Ley de Brandolini"

## M

### Máscara (Mask)
**Definition:** The version of yourself you perform instead of the version that's real.
**Cost:** Increases with scale until the gap becomes unsustainable.
**Source:** "Quitarse la máscara"

### Maestría
**Definition:** Mastery through repeated, intelligent execution.
**vs:** Complexity (which often masks a lack of skill).
**Source:** "Maestría simple"

### Mapa (Map)
**Definition:** The documented/planned version of a territory.
**vs:** The territory (reality, what actually exists).
**Source:** "Dragones en el mapa", "La falacia del cartógrafo"

## N

### Navegante Inmóvil
**Definition:** Someone with knowledge and maps but no movement; a theorist without execution.
**Source:** "Dragones en el mapa"

## O

### Observador (Observer)
**Definition:** Someone who waits for perfect information before acting.
**vs:** Creator (who moves with incomplete information).
**Source:** "Los que crean vs observan"

### Oportunidad (Opportunity)
**Definition:** A potential that exists only for those prepared to see it.
**Principle:** You can't see what you're not prepared to recognize.
**Source:** "Ciego a la oportunidad"

### Optimización
**Definition:** Making something work better but not fundamentally differently.
**Danger:** It's addictive and feels productive while preventing real growth.
**Source:** "Por qué optimizar no es suficiente"

## P

### Paradoja de Easterlin
**Definition:** Beyond basic needs, wealth and happiness are weakly correlated.
**Implication:** Money stops mattering once you have enough; choose differently after that threshold.
**Source:** "Paradoja de Easterlin"

### Paradoja de Teseo
**Definition:** If you change every part of a ship, is it still the same ship?
**Application:** Rapid transformation means loss; decide if it's worth it.
**Source:** "La paradoja de Teseo"

### Parálisis Disfrazada
**Definition:** High activity that creates zero compounding; looking busy without moving.
**Recognition:** You're working hard but results aren't compounding.
**Source:** "Parálisis disfrazada"

### Paciencia vs Talento
**Definition:** Which matters more: natural ability or time invested?
**Answer:** Patience (time invested) almost always beats initial talent.
**Source:** "Paciencia vs talento"

### Paciencia Estratégica
**Definition:** Patience that doesn't depend on willpower, but on how your business is structured.
**The Sequence:** Low costs → margin → time → options.
**vs:** Paciencia como virtud (forcing discipline).
**Principle:** The entrepreneur with most patience isn't the most disciplined; it's the one with lowest burn rate.
**Source:** "El mercado de los limones + Paciencia no se practica + Matemática del intento"

### Permiso Retroactivo
**Definition:** You don't ask for permission; you act, and if it works, permission follows.
**Principle:** Authority is granted by results, not bestowed in advance.
**Source:** "Permiso retroactivo"

## Q

### Quitarse la Máscara
**Definition:** Removing the performed version and showing the real one.
**Requires:** Courage and a sense that the mask is costing more than its protection.
**Source:** "Quitarse la máscara"

## R

### Rechazo (Rejection)
**Definition:** Saying no to things, people, and opportunities that don't align.
**Power:** Every yes to something is a no to everything else.
**Source:** "Por qué rechazar clientes"

## S

### Selección Adversa
**Definition:** When information asymmetry causes quality sellers to leave a market.
**The Mechanism:** Buyers offer middle price → good sellers leave → market fills with lemons → prices fall → more good sellers leave → market destroys itself.
**Application:** In knowledge work, reputation is what prevents commoditization. Signals (portfolio, cases, references) are essential.
**Source:** "El mercado de los limones + Paciencia no se practica + Matemática del intento"

### Sistema (System)
**Definition:** The structures, processes, and decision principles that allow work to happen without you.
**Levels:** Level 1 = you are the system. Level 2 = you direct the system. Level 3 = the system operates with your vision without you.
**Source:** "Facturas como empresario, decides como freelance"

### Sombra (Shadow)
**Definition:** The part of yourself you don't acknowledge but that shows up in your behavior.
**Principle:** Your shadow executes before your conscious mind can intervene.
**Source:** "Tu sombra te delata"

### Suficiente (Enough)
**Definition:** The point where more effort produces diminishing returns.
**vs:** Relentless optimization (which is usually unnecessary past this point).
**Source:** "Suficiente es relativo"

## T

### Talento (Talent)
**Definition:** Natural ability or predisposition.
**Reality:** Usually overstated. Repetition and patience matter more.
**Source:** "Paciencia vs talento"

### Tensión (Tension)
**Definition:** The gap between where you are and where you're trying to go.
**Principle:** Tension is information. It's telling you something needs to change.
**Source:** "Tensión es señal"

### Teseo (Theseus)
**Definition:** The ship in the paradox about whether a ship that's replaced every part is still the same ship.
**Application:** How much can you change and still be you?
**Source:** "La paradoja de Teseo"

### Trabajo Nuevo
**Definition:** Work that doesn't exist yet; what requires you to build without a marked path.
**Characteristic:** Feels harder because there's no routine, no autopilot. Mental effort is enormous.
**vs:** Trabajo viejo (feels productive but is incremental).
**Principle:** New work compounds. Old work just multiplies your current limitations.
**Invest:** 30 minutes per day in new work, pulled from old work. Highest-return investment possible.
**Source:** "Fijación funcional + Trabajo viejo vs trabajo nuevo + Certeza ciega"

### Trabajo Viejo
**Definition:** Work you already know how to do; habitual, what you've executed for years.
**Characteristic:** Feels productive, generates revenue, but is incremental. You feel exhausted but mind is on autopilot.
**The Trap:** Market rewards what you've mastered, not what you're building. So incentive is to stay here.
**Recognition:** When you're productive and results are stagnating.
**Source:** "Fijación funcional + Trabajo viejo vs trabajo nuevo + Certeza ciega"

### Tirar la Escalera
**Definition:** Removing your fallback options to force commitment and creativity.
**Principle:** Half-commitment kills ventures faster than honest failure.
**Source:** "Tirar la escalera"

### Transformación (Transformation)
**Definition:** Fundamental change in structure or nature.
**vs:** Optimization (getting better at what you already do).
**Source:** "Movimientos que transforman", "La puerta de transformación"

## U

### Umbral (Threshold)
**Definition:** A boundary you must cross to reach the next phase or level.
**Principle:** Some thresholds don't repeat; if you don't cross them now, you may not get another chance.
**Source:** "Cruzar umbrales"

## V

### Velocidad (Velocity)
**Definition:** Not just speed, but moving right; progress with direction.
**Principle:** Velocity compounds; direction matters more than speed.
**Source:** All essays; foundational principle

### Ventana (Window)
**Definition:** A temporary opening for action or change.
**Principle:** Not all windows stay open forever.
**Source:** "Tu única ventana"

## Z

### Zajonc (Effect)
**Definition:** Repeated exposure increases preference.
**Use:** Build through repetition, not one-off brilliance.
**Source:** "Efecto Zajonc"

---

**Meta-principle:** Words in this glossary aren't academic. They're tools. Use them to recognize patterns in your own life.
