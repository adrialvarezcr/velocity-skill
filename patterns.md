# VELOCITY Patterns & Techniques

Actionable methods, step-by-step techniques, and decision frameworks extracted from the essays. These aren't abstract concepts—they're moves you can make today.

## Pattern: Imperfect Launch Sequence

**When to use:** You're ready to ship but waiting for perfection.
**Time required:** 1-2 weeks
**Outcome:** Live product + real feedback

### Steps:
1. **Identify the 70% version** (core value, nothing else)
2. **Launch to small group** (friends, beta list, early customers)
3. **Measure 3 metrics:**
   - Is anyone using it?
   - Are they getting value?
   - What are they asking for next?
4. **Iterate based on #3** (not your internal standards)
5. **Expand gradually** (not a big bang)

**Anti-pattern:** Waiting until you have 100% before asking anyone.

## Pattern: Decision Ladder

**When to use:** You're confused about what decision type this is.
**Time required:** 5 minutes
**Outcome:** Clarity on how to proceed

### Decision Types:

**Type 1: Reversible (can undo)**
- Examples: Technology choice, process, tool
- Decision approach: Fast, with intentional review point
- Timeline: Decide today, review in 30 days
- Cost of wrong choice: Low

**Type 2: Difficult to reverse (expensive to undo)**
- Examples: Hiring, capital allocation, big pivots
- Decision approach: Slow, with research and stakeholder input
- Timeline: Decide in 2 weeks after intensive analysis
- Cost of wrong choice: High

**Type 3: Irreversible (can't undo)**
- Examples: Burning your escape routes, burning bridges, fundamental architecture decisions
- Decision approach: Slowest, with deep conviction and alignment
- Timeline: Decide in 4+ weeks, with full team buy-in
- Cost of wrong choice: Existential

### Your job:
Classify every decision into one of these three. Then use the appropriate tempo.

**Common mistake:** Using Type 3 tempo for Type 1 decisions (over-analysis of easy reversals).

## Pattern: Anti-Pattern Recognition

**When to use:** You sense something is wrong but can't articulate it.
**Time required:** 10 minutes
**Outcome:** Diagnosis + action

### The Anti-Pattern Recognition Checklist:

**Signs of Optimization Trap:**
- [ ] Metrics improving but revenue flat
- [ ] Team busy but growth invisible
- [ ] Conversations about "efficiency" instead of "growth"
- [ ] Same focus for 3+ months

**Signs of Perfection Trap:**
- [ ] "Almost ready" said more than once
- [ ] Requirements growing weekly
- [ ] Only you understand what's missing
- [ ] Launch date has slipped

**Signs of Consensus Trap:**
- [ ] Everyone agrees on the direction
- [ ] No real pushback or discomfort
- [ ] You're optimizing, not exploring
- [ ] Growth has plateaued

**Signs of Scale Trap:**
- [ ] Hired people before proving model
- [ ] Managing instead of building
- [ ] Revenue per person declining
- [ ] Can't explain why 10x resources = 10x growth

### Action if you recognize one:
- **Optimization Trap:** Freeze optimization for 30 days. Explore instead.
- **Perfection Trap:** Hard launch date 2 weeks out. Ship on that date.
- **Consensus Trap:** Do something that makes someone uncomfortable.
- **Scale Trap:** Go back to unit economics. Scale only what works.

## Pattern: The Architecture Alignment Check

**When to use:** Before committing to a new project or direction.
**Time required:** 15 minutes
**Outcome:** Clarity on whether this fits your 10-year vision

### Process:

1. **Write your 10-year architecture** (what kind of venture/impact/freedom do you want in 10 years?)
2. **For each new opportunity:**
   - Does it move toward that architecture? (YES → pursue)
   - Does it move away? (NO → decline)
   - Is it unclear? (UNCLEAR → clarify your architecture first)

3. **Rule:** If something is clearly misaligned, decline immediately.

**Principle:** Every yes is a no to everything else. Every no frees up energy for your actual direction.

## Pattern: The Curiosity to Conviction Path

**When to use:** You have an idea but don't know if you should invest in it.
**Time required:** 2-4 weeks
**Outcome:** Data-driven conviction or confident declination

### Stages:

**Stage 1: Curiosity (Week 1)**
- Do minimal research
- Talk to 3-5 people who know this problem
- Read 1-2 good articles
- **Output:** "This might be something"

**Stage 2: Hypothesis (Week 2)**
- Form a specific testable assumption
- Design the smallest experiment to test it
- **Output:** "Here's what needs to be true for this to work"

**Stage 3: Experimentation (Weeks 3-4)**
- Run your experiment with limited resources
- Measure if your hypothesis holds
- **Output:** Evidence for or against your hypothesis

**Stage 4: Decision (Day 28)**
- If evidence supports it → commit and build
- If evidence contradicts it → decline and redeploy resources
- If evidence is mixed → run another experiment (go back to Stage 2)

**Principle:** Evidence beats intuition. Experiments beat planning.

## Pattern: Phase Transitions

**When to use:** You're moving to a new phase of business or capability.
**Time required:** 30-60 days
**Outcome:** Successful transition without losing momentum

### What Happens at Phase Transitions:

- Your old playbook stops working
- Your competitive advantage becomes irrelevant
- You need new skills and mindsets
- Speed and scrappiness become limitations (you need systems)

### How to Transition:

1. **Recognize you're in a new phase** (this is hard; you're usually blind to it)
2. **Identify what worked in the old phase** (honor it, but don't repeat it)
3. **Learn the new phase's rules** (by doing, not studying)
4. **Hire for capabilities you lack** (not to replace you; to extend you)
5. **Iterate the new model** (the playbook won't be perfect; it won't be)

**Principle:** Phase 1 playbooks kill phase 2 businesses. Know which phase you're in.

## Pattern: The Bias Recognition Loop

**When to use:** You keep making the same mistake.
**Time required:** Ongoing
**Outcome:** Conscious correction instead of repeat pattern

### Process:

1. **Identify your bias** (you tend toward optimization/perfection/consensus/etc.)
2. **Name it** (this makes it visible)
3. **Create a counter-move** (if you bias toward caution, commit first; if you bias toward recklessness, plan first)
4. **Apply the counter-move consciously** (it will feel wrong; that's how you know it's working)
5. **Review and iterate** (did the counter-move work? Or do you need a different one?)

**Principle:** You can't eliminate your biases. You can become aware of them and compensate.

## Pattern: The Compounding Check

**When to use:** You've been working on something 6+ months and the results feel flat.
**Time required:** 30 minutes
**Outcome:** Clarity on whether this will compound or whether you should stop

### Questions:

1. **Are results accelerating?** (Should be 2-3x better than month 1 by month 6)
2. **Is learning compounding?** (Should be finding better moves, not just repeating)
3. **Is your leverage increasing?** (Should be able to do more with less effort)
4. **Are you still learning or just grinding?** (Learning should feel hard; grinding feels repetitive)

### Decision Matrix:

| Acceleration | Learning | Leverage | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| YES | YES | YES | ACCELERATE |
| YES | YES | NO | Keep going, need system-building |
| YES | NO | NO | You're on an exhaustion trajectory—pace yourself |
| NO | YES | NO | You're learning but not scaling—refine |
| NO | NO | NO | STOP—this isn't compounding. Redeploy. |

**Principle:** Compounding requires three things. Without all three, you're just grinding.

## Pattern: Threshold Recognition

**When to use:** You sense an important moment is passing.
**Time required:** Immediate (or never)
**Outcome:** Cross the threshold or accept the consequence

### Thresholds in Ventures:

- **Product-Market Fit Threshold:** Once crossed, growth is inevitable. Not crossed, death is inevitable.
- **Team Conviction Threshold:** Once crossed, execution accelerates. Not crossed, execution stalls.
- **Market Timing Threshold:** Some moments repeat. Many don't. This one might not.
- **Capability Threshold:** You need to become X kind of person to go to the next phase. This is your moment to transform.

### How to Recognize a Threshold:

- It feels urgent and final
- Waiting feels like it closes a door, not opens one
- You're aware of it only for a short window
- On the other side, the game is different

### Your job:
**Recognize it. Decide. Cross or accept the consequence.**

**Principle:** Some doors open only once. Know which ones.

## Core Principle Across All Patterns

**Execute before you understand. Understanding follows execution.**

All these patterns are tools. The only tool that matters is action.
