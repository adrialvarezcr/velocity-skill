# VELOCITY Quick-Reference Cheatsheet

Decision tables, action matrices, and quick-reference rules. When you're in the heat of execution and need to know NOW.

## When Should You...?

### ...Launch?
| Condition | Decision | Principle |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Waiting for perfection** | LAUNCH NOW | Perfect is the enemy of shipped. Launch 70%, iterate from feedback. |
| **Building 6+ months** | LAUNCH NOW | If it's taking this long, you've lost the market. |
| **"Just 2 more weeks"** | LAUNCH NOW | This is how projects die. Ship what you have. |
| **Team unanimously says yes** | PAUSE | Unanimous agreement signals you're not exploring enough. |

### ...Pivot?
| Condition | Decision | Principle |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Market feedback says wrong problem** | PIVOT | You can't be too attached to your first idea. |
| **You're the only believer** | STAY | Conviction beats consensus early. |
| **Phase has fundamentally changed** | PIVOT | Old rules don't work in new phases. |
| **Competitors are ahead but different path exists** | STAY | Your differentiation is in how you move, not where you're going. |

### ...Optimize?
| Condition | Decision | Principle |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **You have product-market fit** | OPTIMIZE | Only optimize what's already working. |
| **You're still searching for PMF** | STOP OPTIMIZING | Every day optimizing is a day not exploring. |
| **You've found 3 growth levers** | OPTIMIZE | Optimize one at a time, deeply. |
| **You can't name your top 3 metrics** | STOP OPTIMIZING | You're guessing. Go back to basics. |

### ...Say No?
| Condition | Decision | Principle |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Client doesn't align with your product vision** | SAY NO | Misaligned clients create debt, not revenue. |
| **Opportunity seems interesting but not strategic** | SAY NO | Every yes is a no to your actual priorities. |
| **Your capacity is full** | SAY NO | Overcommitment kills execution velocity. |
| **It's easier to say yes** | SAY NO | Ease now = pain later. |

### ...Persist?
| Condition | Decision | Principle |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **You're seeing progress (even small)** | PERSIST | Compounding requires you to stay through the flat part. |
| **Market is changing but direction is right** | PERSIST | You're not fighting entropy; you're navigating it. |
| **Everyone says you're crazy but you have evidence** | PERSIST | Breakthrough ideas look insane before they're obvious. |
| **You're tired, not stuck** | PERSIST | Fatigue is not signal. Results are signal. |
| **You've been here 3 years with no progress** | QUIT | Sunk cost is not a reason to stay. |

### ...Scale?
| Condition | Decision | Principle |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Unit economics work** | SCALE | You've proven the model. Now multiply it. |
| **Growth is linear, not compounding** | DON'T SCALE | You'll scale your mistakes instead of your wins. |
| **You need to hire to grow** | SCALE | People are your multiplier. |
| **You're the only one who understands the business** | DON'T SCALE | Scaling requires systems, not charisma. |

## Decision Matrices by Situation

### Stuck on a Decision?

**"Should I stay or should I go?"**
```
Is there evidence of progress?
  ├─ YES, clear signal → STAY + ACCELERATE
  ├─ YES, but slow → STAY + REFOCUS (reduce variables)
  ├─ NO, but learning fast → STAY + PERSIST (you're early)
  ├─ NO, and learning slow → QUIT + REDEPLOY (to something else)
  └─ UNCLEAR → ACTIVATE (do something measurable today)
```

**"Do I need permission to start?"**
```
Do you need anyone's money?
  ├─ YES → Get to MVP first, THEN pitch
  ├─ NO → SHIP + ASK FORGIVENESS LATER
```

**"Is this a real opportunity or am I chasing shiny?"**
```
Does it fit your architecture?
  ├─ YES, aligned with 10-year vision → PURSUE
  ├─ YES, aligned with 1-year plan → PURSUE
  ├─ NO, cool but off-strategy → DECLINE
  └─ UNCLEAR → Do ONE EXPERIMENT (don't commit resources)
```

## Patterns to Recognize (Anti-Patterns in the Wild)

### You're in the Optimization Trap if:
- [ ] You're improving metrics but revenue isn't moving
- [ ] You can't name the ONE thing you're optimizing for
- [ ] You've been "optimizing" the same thing for 3+ months
- [ ] Your team is busy but growth is flat

**Escape:** Freeze optimization for 30 days. Explore instead.

### You're in the Perfection Trap if:
- [ ] You say "almost ready to launch" more than once
- [ ] You're finding new requirements weekly
- [ ] Your launch date has slipped 3+ times
- [ ] Only you can explain why it's not ready

**Escape:** Set a hard launch date 2 weeks from today. Ship on that date.

### You're in the Consensus Trap if:
- [ ] Everyone on your team agrees it's a good idea
- [ ] You're not hearing real pushback or discomfort
- [ ] You're optimizing instead of exploring
- [ ] Growth has plateaued

**Escape:** Intentionally do something that will make someone uncomfortable. You need friction.

### You're in the Scale Trap if:
- [ ] You hired people before proving the business model
- [ ] You're managing instead of building
- [ ] Revenue per person is declining
- [ ] You can't articulate why 10x more resources = 10x more growth

**Escape:** Go back to the unit economics. Scale only what works.

### You're in the Distraction Trap if:
- [ ] You're working 80 hours and results are 40 hours
- [ ] You have 5+ "priorities"
- [ ] You're executing but progress is invisible
- [ ] Every week you're on a different problem

**Escape:** Choose ONE metric. Make everything else subordinate.

## The 3 Questions You Should Ask Yourself Every Week

1. **Is this moving toward my 10-year architecture, or away from it?**
   - If away: Quit it this week.
   - If toward: Keep accelerating.
   - If unclear: Clarify your 10-year vision first.

2. **Am I executing or optimizing?**
   - Executing = Creating new capability or reaching new people
   - Optimizing = Making something work better
   - You need both, but executing comes first.
   - If you've been optimizing 2+ months, it's time to execute.

3. **What am I not seeing because I'm not prepared to see it?**
   - This is your blind spot.
   - It's invisible to you because you lack the framework or the commitment to understand it.
   - Find one person who sees clearly what you don't. Listen.

## Execution Velocity Checkpoint

**Score yourself (1-5, where 5 is "completely true"):**

- [ ] **I ship imperfect work regularly** (1-5): ___
- [ ] **I say no strategically** (1-5): ___
- [ ] **I know my blind spots** (1-5): ___
- [ ] **I don't optimize prematurely** (1-5): ___
- [ ] **I distinguish between momentum and productivity** (1-5): ___
- [ ] **I'm comfortable being uncomfortable** (1-5): ___
- [ ] **I act without waiting for permission** (1-5): ___

**Total: ___ / 35**

- **28-35:** You're operating at velocity. Keep shipping.
- **21-27:** You're mostly there. Identify 1 blocker and remove it.
- **14-20:** You're stuck. You're probably optimizing instead of executing. Reset.
- **Below 14:** You're in analysis mode. Ship something imperfect this week.

## The Velocity Ladder

**Where are you?**

1. **Stuck** — Haven't started or haven't shipped anything yet
   - Action: Launch imperfect version this week
   
2. **Moving** — Shipping regularly, getting feedback, iterating
   - Action: Identify what's slowing you down. Remove it.
   
3. **Accelerating** — Growth is compounding, team is aligned, execution is smooth
   - Action: Double down on what's working. Find the edge.
   
4. **Leading** — You're defining the game. Others are following your moves.
   - Action: Stay ahead of your own success becoming your ceiling.

---

**Remember:** Velocity isn't about speed. It's about moving right. All the speed in the world toward the wrong destination is just wasting fuel faster.
